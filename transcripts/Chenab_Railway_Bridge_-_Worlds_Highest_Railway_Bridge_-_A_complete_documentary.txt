The triumph of this bridge, it delved deep into the slopes on either side of the valley. Stabilizing these slopes required meticulous effort and innovative techniques. A team of experts from various institutes such as AICTE, IISC Bengaluru, IIT Delhi was roped in for stoope stabilization process. The teams studied the rocks and samples for geological analysis. Presence of fractures, fissures, etc. This helped in determining how the stability of these slopes can be maintained without any vulnerabilities. Analysis was done using many methods as entire slope was looked at as one mass. A special kind of a modeling was done to understand all possible stability issues. It was a stepbystep approach patiently executed by the team. The bridge has two major foundations S40 and S50. These not only take the whole load of the bridge but also of the trains flying on it. Cutting edge dip and switch software were used to analyze slope stability under heavy loads. These foundations are the springing points of the arch and hence they were crucial for the project success. The ancient and steep slopes of the Himalayas were tamed using massive consolidation grouting, making them sturdy for the colossal arch foundations. A series of rock and cable anchors bolstered foundation stability. Extreme care has been taken at each step to ensure a worldclass structure. The viaduct construction was another unique challenge. The vioaduct starts at one end on the core side and goes straight for a certain length. Then it enters a transition curve followed again by a straight line over the arch before entering the buckle side. Thus, there were multiple transition areas. Keeping this in mind, launching sequence was meticulously planned in four sections. First the launching on transition curve was achieved from core side which is a unique and firsttime feature in the construction industry. Then from another foundation with the help of a platform balanced via duct straight portion was reverse launched to meet the already launched curved portion. [Music] Subsequently, the segments were incrementally pushed from both core and buckle ends till they came and met in the middle of the crown of the arch, which was called as the golden joint.
